Portrait of Adele Bloch-Bauer I

“Portrait of Adele Bloch-Bauer I” also known as “The Lady in Gold” or “The Woman in Gold” is an oil painting with gold leaf by Gustav Klimt. It was completed between 1903 and 1907.

The portrait shows Adele Bloch-Bauer sitting on a golden throne, with a glowing star-like golden background behind her. She wears a jewelled necklace that is similar to the one Klimt used in his painting “Judith”. Her dress is tight and golden, shaped in a triangular form and designed with geometric, straight-lined patterns.

The artwork was stolen by the Nazis in 1941 and was later displayed at the Österreichische Galerie Belvedere. This painting is considered the most important and final work of Klimt’s golden phase
