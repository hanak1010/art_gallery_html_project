Girl with a Pearl Earring

Girl with a Pearl Earring, is an oil painting by Dutch Golden Age painter Johannes Vermeer which dates back to c. 1665.

The painting is considered a tronie, a 17th-century Dutch term used for studies of a face or character rather than a formal portrait. It shows a European girl dressed in what was seen as exotic clothing, including an oriental style turban and a strikingly large pearl earring. The identity of the girl remains unknown, she could have been a real person or a product of the artist’s imagination, perhaps possibly intended to represent a Sibyl or a figure from the Bible.

The painting has been part of the Mauritshuis collection in The Hague since 1902, and over time it has inspired numerous works in both literature and film.
