Ophelia

“Ophelia” (1851–52) is an oil painting by British artist John Everett Millais. It is part of the Tate Britain collection in London.

The painting shows Ophelia, a young noblewoman from William Shakespeare’s play Hamlet. Because of Hamlet’s actions, she loses her sanity and eventually drowns. This artwork is considered one of the most important paintings of the mid-19th century and is known for its beauty and detailed natural setting. The scene shows Ophelia floating in a river while singing, just before her death. This moment comes from Act IV, Scene VII of Hamlet where Queen Gertrude describes what happened.

The flowers around Ophelia in the water match Shakespeare’s description of the garland she was wearing. They also connect to the Victorian idea of the “language of flowers,” where each flower has its own symbolic meaning.
