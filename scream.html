The Scream

“The Scream” is an artwork created in 1893 by Norwegian artist Edvard Munch. It's original Norwegian title is Skrik (“Scream”) and was first shown in Germany under the name Der Schrei der Natur (“The Scream of Nature”).

The painting shows a figure with a distressed face, which has become one of the most famous images in art. It is often seen as expressing deep fear and anxiety connected to human life. Munch’s work played an important role in shaping the Expressionist movement. The artwork was made using oil, tempera, pastel and crayon on cardboard.

Today it is considered a symbol of modern art, sometimes compared to the “Mona Lisa” of our time and is often understood as representing the shared anxiety of people in the modern world
